Research profile

Clinically grounded research on circulatory monitoring and perioperative outcomes

The group works at the intersection of anaesthesiology, intensive care, cardiac surgery, high-risk noncardiac surgery, physiology and method-comparison statistics.

Research approach

From clinical monitoring signals to patient-relevant outcomes

We combine pragmatic clinical datasets, prospective method-comparison studies and physiologic reasoning to evaluate how hemodynamic variables should be measured, interpreted and used in perioperative and critical care.

Principal investigator

Timo Kaakinen

MD, PhD, Adjunct Professor of Anaesthesiology and Intensive Care

The research group has been led by Dr Kaakinen since 2017. The central research focus is the clinical reliability and interpretation of hemodynamic monitoring methods and the association between circulatory variables and patient-centered outcomes.

Current research combines large retrospective clinical datasets, prospective method-comparison studies, high-risk perioperative cohorts and intensive care data from Oulu University Hospital.

Research methods

How we evaluate monitoring data

Methodology is a central part of the group’s work. We focus on whether a measurement is reliable enough for clinical use, not only on whether two variables are statistically associated.

Research portfolio

Core study lines

The group’s projects form a portfolio that spans perioperative monitoring validation, pulmonary artery catheter data, oxygen delivery, dynamic variables, anaesthesia methods and point-of-care monitoring.

Research collaboration

Collaborative clinical and translational research

The group collaborates with clinical departments, academic partners and selected external partners in studies where perioperative physiology, monitoring data and patient outcomes meet.

Clinical research environment

The new Oulu University Hospital campus. Image: OYS / Oulu University Hospital.

The group is based at Oulu University Hospital, a tertiary academic hospital in Northern Finland. The research environment enables clinically relevant studies using perioperative, cardiac surgical and intensive care data, with strong connections to anaesthesia, intensive care, cardiac surgery, vascular surgery and high-risk perioperative medicine.
